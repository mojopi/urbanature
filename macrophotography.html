iphone macrophotography

I bought a macro lens for my iphone and went to Mt. Auburn Cemetary to try it out. Here are the things I learned and the prompts I generated.



Prompts:

eyes
skeletons
flowers
ants
water droplets
lichen
fungi
underwater

Things I learned:

1. I move and plants move...constantly. Use a hard surface (eg. a gravestone or tree) to lean on. Or, sit on the ground and lean arms on crossed legs to take a photograph of something below the camera. This is much easier than taking a photo of something at eye level where you don't have anything to lean on.
Macrophotography with an iphone is more like yoga than I thought.

2. I can use myself as a tripod. I sat on a rock balanced my head on a wet log I found to take pictures of frogs in a pond.

3. The depth of field on my iphone is very limited and the amount of an organism that I can actually capture in a focused way is...not much.
