scan + cyan

You could imagine using various tools and materials to see plants and animals in new ways. Recently, I got a flatbed scanner and have been scanning different types of flowers, leaves, and seeds to make high resolution images. The scanner allows me to capture a perspective that I would never be able to secure with a traditional camera.

You could imagine using this tool to make, interactive, digital stories with images of seaweed, a photography book about feathers, grasses, or the small flowers we rarely notice, a stationary line with images of local plants on the front and small descriptions on the back, etc.

The idea of scanning plants was inspired by this beautiful book about seaweed called The Ocean Garden. Seaweed seems like a particularly good medium to scan because it is flat and transparent. I've found that flat plant and anima parts (eg. ferns leaves, grass flowers, feathers) are also well-suited to this tool.

I've also started to make cyanotypes (blue prints where that use chemical reactions with UV light). I'm particularly excited about making prints (or maybe a book) of entire plants and root systems this way.
