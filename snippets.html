walking and seeing Go on walk with a friend by a river or park for at least an hour. As you're walking, photograph things you find interesting. Here are some prompts. Take photographs of: Something fuzzy Something that looks different when you get really close Something that reveals something new when you hold it up to the sun An textured surface A Pattern Something made of angular edges and lines Something made of curves and circles

shared reference accretion For a month or several, every time you see a certain type of organism (eg. bees, grapes, tomatoes) or a phenomenon (eg. moonrise, oily puddles that make rainbows, certain shadows) take a photograph of it [and text it to the same person]. Each time you take a photograph, try to see and represent the thing in new ways. For instance, if you're photographing a particular kind of plant, zoom in on a certain part (eg. the veins of it's leaf or the top of it's fruit) or take it from a new perspective (eg. from the underside of a leaf). After several months, take what you've photographed and make something with the materials you generated. You can imagine using the images as part of a digital story, an interactive map, a book that you print, an exhibit, something that's responsive to an API or a sensor, etc. useful tools: day 1

sketching Find a plant or a small area that appeals to you and sketch it [in different ways and from different perspectives] for an hour. useful tools: paper
