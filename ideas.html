Walk dogs and get to know them without seeing them as cute. Journal about them. Draw them. Observe them. Learn how to "be in the same play" as them. If they're cowring in corner, what do you do? how do you act? If they're barking and nipping, what feeling do you project?

When starting to sketch and watercolor with YPs, only use a small (fits in hand) sketchbook to limit your frame and to make sure that you focus on a small area and really see the details. Similarly, when working with flowers start with macro flowers like zuchinni flowers (they are particularly good), not compound flowers like lilacs because even though you want to focus on detail, you want to get the gist and the form of the thing and not keep everything tight and small.
Source: drawing a flower a day and doing watercolor in Amagansett.
